He was the runner up on the second season of ''[[Nashville Star]]'' in 2004.  He grew up in Fox Brook, [[Pictou County]], Nova Scotia before he moved west to [[Calgary|Calgary, Alberta]]. He also holds an appointment in the Canadian Forces as the Colonel Commandant of the [[Royal Canadian Air Cadets]].<ref name=\"thechronicleherald.ca\">{{cite news |title=Canyon honoured by air force cadets  |url=http://thechronicleherald.ca/novascotia/46097-across-nova-scotia |newspaper=[[The Chronicle Herald]] |date=December 24, 2011 |access-date=May 18, 2024 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120111215355/http://thechronicleherald.ca/novascotia/46097-across-nova-scotia |archivedate=January 11, 2012}}</ref>\n\n==Biography==\nGeorge Canyon has held many jobs: he spent time working as a bylaw enforcement officer/special constable, a [[slaughterhouse]] beef inspector, and the owner/operator of his own recording studio. He always dreamed of becoming a professional musician.<ref>url={{cite web |url=http://www.georgecanyon.com/about |title=About &#124; Official Website of George Canyon:Multiple Juno and CCMA Award Winning Canadian Country Artist |access-date=August 25, 2010 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100828081334/http://www.georgecanyon.com/about |archive-date=August 28, 2010 }}</ref>\n    \nCanyon is the father of two children. His wife worked three jobs during the day so that he could sing at night. In 2004, George Canyon competed in ''[[Nashville Star]] 2''. He was the runner-up in the competition, but became very well known in Canada and garnered much support, being the only Canadian to make the cut for the competition. After the competition ended, he received many accolades from fans and the industry alike. These include the top 5 hit \"My Name\", a [[Juno Award]] for Best Country Recording of the Year (for his album ''[[One Good Friend]]''), and, four [[Canadian Country Music Awards]] Nominations (CCMAs) including Male Vocalist of the Year and the Fan Choice Award.\n\nOn September 22, 2007, Canyon performed at ''China-Canada: Hand in Hand'';<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=xMrWYKUT7No# YouTube \u2013 Suzie McNeil \u2013 China-Canada: Hand in Hand<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> a joint concert held in [[Vancouver]] between [[Chinese Central Television|CCTV]] (China Central Television) and [[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|CBC]] (Canadian Broadcasting Corporation) to commemorate the one-year countdown of the 2008 Beijing Olympics.  One of the songs performed by Canyon was \"What a Fine Jasmine Blossom\". Sung entirely in [[Mandarin Chinese|Mandarin]], Canyon performed the duet with Mandarin singer Tang Can.<ref>[https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=inXXkLw00K0 YouTube \u2013 George Canyon sings in Mandarin \u2013 Hand in Hand<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref>\n\nOn January 9, 2008, Canadian defence minister [[Peter MacKay]] appointed Canyon to the position of honorary colonel at [[14 Wing Greenwood]], the largest air base on the east coast.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.cbc.ca/news/arts/meet-col-canyon-the-country-star-1.720155|title= Meet Col. Canyon, the country star|publisher=Canadian Broadcasting Corporation| access-date=October 2, 2015 | date=January 10, 2008}}</ref> After this appointment expired in December 2011, Canyon was appointed colonel commandant of the [[Royal Canadian Air Cadets]].<ref name=\"thechronicleherald.ca\"/>\n \nCanyon was diagnosed with [[Diabetes mellitus type 1|type 1 diabetes]] at the age of 14 and has given talks to children about diabetes.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.georgecanyon.com/diabetes.html |title=Dead link |work=The Official Community of George Canyon |access-date=May 16, 2008 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080514161925/http://www.georgecanyon.com/diabetes.html |archive-date=May 14, 2008 |url-status=dead }}</ref>\n\nOn October 16, 2014, Canyon was named the official anthem singer for the [[Calgary Flames]] of the [[National Hockey League]].<ref>{{cite news|title=George Canyon named 2014\u201315 Flames anthem singer|url=http://flames.nhl.com/club/news.htm?id=734780|publisher=CalgaryFlames.com}}</ref>\n\nIn 2020, Canyon appeared as a panelist on ''[[Canada Reads]]'', advocating for [[Jesse Thistle]]'s memoir ''From the Ashes''.<ref>[https://www.cbc.ca/books/canadareads/meet-the-canada-reads-2020-contenders-1.5433115 \"Meet the Canada Reads 2020 contenders\"]. [[CBC Books]], January 22, 2020.</ref>\n\nIn 2022, Canyon published a memoir entitled ''My Country'', in which he describes his musical career from [[Nova Scotia]] to [[Nashville]] and back.<ref>[https://www.cbc.ca/books/george-canyon-s-memoir-my-country-is-about-hard-times-hard-work-and-good-music-read-an-excerpt-now-1.6486325 \"George Canyon's memoir My Country is about hard times, hard work and good music \u2014 read an excerpt now\"]. [[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation|CBC]], June 13, 2022.</ref>\n\n==Acting==\nIn addition to his singing career, Canyon has also performed as an actor in movies and television. His first role of note was as \"Head Forest Ranger\" in several episodes during the seventh season of the popular [[Showcase (Canadian TV channel)|Showcase]] television series ''[[Trailer Park Boys]]'' in 2007. He also appeared as the character \"Charlie Wells\" in the series ''[[Heartland (Canadian TV series)|Heartland]]'' in 2008. His most notable film role to date was as the character \"Cattle Jack\" in the 2012 film ''[[Dawn Rider]]'', a remake of a 1935 [[John Wayne]] film of the same name. He recorded his first album in 1996 titled ''Young in Love''. In 2001, after years of playing clubs in BC and Alberta, Pritchett entered a singing contest called \"Project Discovery\" sponsored by CMT and won a professional music video directed by internationally acclaimed director, Steven Goldmann as well as $10,000 cash. He put the money towards recording his first album, titled \"Consider This\". The title track was co-written by Pritchett and longtime BC Country artist Rick Tippe.\n\nPritchett then released his next two albums ''Something Going On Here'' in 2003 and ''Big Wheel'' in 2006. The latter included the single \"[[Hold My Beer (Aaron Pritchett song)|Hold My Beer]]\", which won Songwriter(s) of the Year at the 2007 [[Canadian Country Music Awards]].<ref>{{Cite news|work=[[CBC News]]|title=Emerson Drive snags three CCMA awards|date=September 10, 2007|url=https://www.cbc.ca/news/entertainment/emerson-drive-snags-three-ccma-awards-1.654320|access-date=December 23, 2023}}</ref>\n\nIn 2008 he was signed to [[604 Records]], the production company of [[Nickelback]]'s [[Chad Kroeger]]. Pritchett toured Western Canada with [[Toby Keith]] and fellow 604 Records artist [[Jessie Farrell]] to promote his album, ''Thankful'', which was released on September 9, 2008.\n\nPritchett released the album ''In the Driver's Seat'' on November 9, 2010, under his own record label Decibel Music. Pritchett's first greatest hits album, ''Body of Work: A Collection of Hits'', was released on May 12, 2015.<ref>{{cite news|title=Aaron Pritchett releases a collection of hits May 12|url=http://www.730ckdm.com/index.php/music/item/5246-aaron-pritchett-releases-a-collection-of-hits-may-12|access-date=April 10, 2015|work=[[CKDM]]|date=April 7, 2015}}</ref> under Big Star Recordings.\n\nIn June 2016, Pritchett released his album ''The Score'', a title that commemorated his 20th year recording and releasing music. The lead off single \"[[Dirt Road in 'Em]]\" went to No. 6 on the charts and marked a comeback in Pritchett's career as it was his first top 10 single since 2009. The second release, \"Out Of The Blue\" followed suit reaching No. 9. The album was nominated for Country Album of the Year at the 2017 Juno Awards. His release \"When A Momma's Boy Meets A Daddy's Girl\" peaked at No. 5 on the Billboard Canada Country chart.\n\nIn April 2019, Pritchett scored his first #1 hit on the ''[[Billboard (magazine)|Billboard]]'' [[Canada Country]] chart with \"[[Better When I Do]]\".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.iegroup.ca/news/aaron-pritchett-receives-1-single-better-when-i-do/ |title=AARON PRITCHETT RECEIVES #1 SINGLE WITH 'BETTER WHEN I DO' TOPPING THE CANADIAN COUNTRY RADIO CHARTS|date=April 8, 2019|work=Invictus Entertainment Group|access-date=December 12, 2020}}</ref>\n\n==Band==\n===Current===\n*Jayson Brinkworth \u2013 Drums\n*John Sponarski \u2013 Guitar, Vocals\n*Scott Smith \u2013 Guitar, Steel Guitar, Vocals\n*Shane Hendrickson \u2013 Bass, Vocals\n*Emil Gawaziuk \u2013 Monitors, stage tech\n*Kirby Barber \u2013 Guitar, vocals\n\n===Former===\n*[[Mitch Merrett]]\n*Jay Buettner\n*Mike Sanyshyn\n*Ron Briggs\n*Mike Norman\n*Dennis Marcenko\n*Darren Parris\n*Bruce Morrison\n*[[Fosterchild|Peter Sweetzir]]\n*Ken Friesen\n\n==Discography==\n===Albums===\n====Studio albums====\n{| class=\"wikitable plainrowheaders\" style=\"text-align:center;\"\n|-\n!
